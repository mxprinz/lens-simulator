Lesson 1: The Portrait

Your Assignment

You need to take a photo of this girl at sunset. To make it look "professional," follow these three rules:

1. Blur the Background
Use a wide Aperture (low f-number) so the girl stands out.

2. Keep it Sharp
Keep the Shutter fast. If it's too slow, the photo will be blurry from your hands shaking.

3. Keep it Clean
Keep the ISO low. High ISO makes the photo look "grainy."

Lesson 2: Fast Action

Your Assignment

It's a bright, sunny day. Let's capture the skater with settings that a pro would actually use in this light.

1. Freeze the Action
Movement this fast requires a very high Shutter speed to stop the blur mid-air.

2. Environmental Clarity
We want the whole park sharp. Use a mid-range Aperture to keep the background in focus rather than blurring it.

3. Daylight Standard
With the sun providing so much light, keep your sensor sensitivity at its lowest base setting for the cleanest shot.

Lesson 3: City Nightscape

Your Assignment

It's twilight, and the city lights are beginning to glow.

1. Sharp Details
We want the historic building and the distant cityscape to stay sharp. Use an Aperture that ensures total clarity from front to back.

2. Handheld Handshake
To keep it steady without a tripod, don't let the Shutter speed drop below 1/60. Slow enough for light, fast enough for focus.

3. High Sensitivity
Twilight is dark. You will need a high sensitivity to capture the scene's ambient light.
